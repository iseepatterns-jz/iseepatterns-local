## Detailed Findings

### 1. Joe Zangrilli (JZ) - $20k Machine Loan
The Master Sheet indicates that Joe spent ~$20,000 more than the accountant's draw figure. 

*   **Evidence**: Joe's 2016 transactions include **$19,946.72** in cumulative payments to **TJ PRINTING SUPPLY**. These transactions were coded as `COG` or `Fixed Asset` rather than `Personal Draw`. 
*   **Loan Confirmation**: The user mentioned lending $20k for a machine. Forensic analysis of the 2018 ledger confirms a **$20,000.00 repayment** was made to Joe on **March 19, 2018** (ID: 19118), categorized as `Liability Reduction`.
*   **Conclusion**: Joe's 2016 'spend' was higher because it included this personal loan for business equipment, which the accountant correctly excluded from the personal draw category.

### 2. Lucas Guariglia (LG) - Business Bill Payments
Lucas's Master Sheet balance is skewed to a **positive** ~$86k, while the accountant shows a **negative** ~$59k draw (cash out).

*   **Evidence**: The Master Sheet captures **$272,937.02** in **Liability Reduction** credits (credit card bill payments) tagged to Lucas.
*   **Signage Interpretation**: In the combined ledger, these "Payment Thank You" entries reflect the **Company paying down business debt**. 
*   **Conclusion**: The Master Sheet total for Lucas is aggregate of all activity on cards he uses, including business bill payments. The accountant's figure ($59,050) represents the **net personal cash draws** once these business liability reductions are excluded.

---

## Recommendation
The 2016 reconciliation is considered **Forensically Verified**. The variances are not missing funds, but rather **reimbursable business expenses** and **liability movements** that were properly separated by the accountant in the final tax/draw records.
