## Section 1: Evidence Summary

This exhibit captures a March 31, 2020 email thread in which Fifth Third Bank AVP Leonard Mayersky initiates a $100,000 "Fundation" loan application on behalf of Rowboat Creative, LLC, copying both co-founders — Joe Zangrilli (COO) and Lucas Guariglia — on the internal bank communication. COO Joe Zangrilli then replies directly to Fifth Third's financial center manager with detailed business metrics (annual sales of $1,961,141 in 2018 and $2,406,195 in 2019, 33 full-time employees, 1,815 customers, $17,544.46 monthly lease), while deferring personal financial disclosures to a separate channel. The timing — four days after the CARES Act was signed into law (March 27, 2020) — places this loan application squarely within the window when Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) and other COVID-relief lending programs were being operationalized, and the exhibit tends to prove that both co-founders had contemporaneous knowledge of and participated in the company's pursuit of bank financing facilitated by a Fifth Third insider.

## Section 2: Claim Alignment

### Fiduciary Breach — SUPPORTING
JZ, as COO and co-founder, is demonstrably acting in his fiduciary capacity by providing company financial data for a loan application. Both co-founders are CC'd, establishing shared contemporaneous knowledge of the financing activity. The exhibit does not itself prove breach but supports the existence of the fiduciary relationship and the financial decision-making context in which alleged breaches occurred. Governing law: *In re Trados Inc. S'holder Litig.*, 73 A.3d 17 (Del. Ch. 2013) (fiduciary duties of officers in LLC context); 805 ILCS 180/15-3 (Illinois LLC Act — fiduciary duties of members and managers).

### Fraud (Common Law) — SUPPORTING
The financial figures JZ provides ($1,961,141 and $2,406,195 in annual sales) are specific representations made to a federally insured financial institution in connection with a loan application. If these figures are materially inconsistent with the company's tax filings, QuickBooks records, or other financial documentation produced in discovery, this email becomes direct evidence of either a false statement to a bank or, alternatively, undisclosed discrepancies in company financial reporting. Governing law: *Doe v. Dilling*, 228 Ill. 2d 324 (2008) (elements: false statement of material fact, knowing falsity, intent to induce reliance, justifiable reliance, damages).

### Wire Fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1343) — SUPPORTING
The email transmission itself uses interstate wire communications (internet email) to transmit financial representations to a bank for the purpose of obtaining a $100,000 loan. If the overarching scheme involved misrepresentations to Fifth Third or misappropriation of loan proceeds, this email constitutes a wire transmission in furtherance of that scheme. Governing law: 18 U.S.C. § 1343; *Bridge v. Phoenix Bond & Indem. Co.*, 553 U.S. 639 (2008) (civil RICO predicate).

### Conversion — BACKGROUND
No direct evidence of misappropriation of funds appears in this exhibit. However, the exhibit establishes that a loan application was pending, which may later prove relevant to tracing the disposition of any loan proceeds.

### Breach of Contract — SUPPORTING
The Rowboat Creative operating agreement likely contains provisions governing major financial decisions, incurrence of debt, and required member consent. This email tends to prove that both members had contemporaneous notice of a $100,000 loan application — relevant to whether the operating agreement's notice, consent, or approval requirements were satisfied or violated. Governing law: 805 ILCS 180/15-1 et seq.; operating agreement (to be introduced).

### Accounting — DIRECT
JZ provides specific, verifiable financial figures: $1,961,141 (2018 annual sales) and $2,406,195 (2019 annual sales). These figures can be directly cross-referenced against the company's QuickBooks records, tax returns (Form 1065 / Schedules K-1), and bank statements. Any material discrepancy between these representations and the company's books constitutes direct evidence relevant to the accounting claim. Governing law: 805 ILCS 180/10-15 (member's right to inspect books and records).

### Unjust Enrichment — BACKGROUND
No direct evidence of benefit conferred or retained. May become relevant if loan proceeds were obtained and diverted.

### RICO (Civil — 18 U.S.C. § 1962) — SUPPORTING
Mayersky's active role as a Fifth Third insider initiating and facilitating the loan application, combined with the "mayersky_smoking_gun" source database designation and the "BANK_FRAUD_TRANSFERS" tag, suggests this exhibit forms part of a broader pattern. If the evidence shows multiple such insider-facilitated transactions across exhibits, this email is one predicate act in a pattern of racketeering activity. Governing law: 18 U.S.C. §§ 1962(c), 1964(c); *H.J. Inc. v. Northwestern Bell Tel. Co.*, 492 U.S. 229 (1989) (pattern requirement).

### Mail Fraud (18 U.S.C. § 1341) — TANGENTIAL
Email is wire communication, not mail. Mail fraud requires use of the U.S. Postal Service or private interstate carrier. No such use is evidenced in this exhibit.

### Computer Fraud (CFAA — 18 U.S.C. § 1030) — TANGENTIAL
No evidence of unauthorized access to a protected computer. The email reflects authorized use of corporate email systems by all participants.

## Section 4: Admissibility Analysis (FRE)

### FRE 401/402 — Relevance: PASS
The email tends to make facts of consequence more probable: (a) both co-founders knew of and participated in the $100K loan application, (b) JZ represented specific financial figures to a bank, (c) Mayersky acted as an insider facilitator, and (d) the loan application was made four days after the CARES Act. All are relevant to claims of fiduciary breach, fraud, accounting, and RICO.

### FRE 403 — Risk of Unfair Prejudice: LOW
The email is a routine business communication. No inflammatory content, no graphic material, no risk of misleading the jury or causing confusion. Probative value is not substantially outweighed by any prejudicial effect. Any risk of confusion about the "Fundation" product name can be cured with a brief explanatory foundation.

### FRE 801/802 — Hearsay Analysis
- **JZ's statements (Message 3):** Admissible under FRE 801(d)(2)(A) as statements of a party-opponent. JZ is the plaintiff. His representations of company financials are admissions.
- **JZ's statements:** Alternatively admissible under FRE 801(d)(2)(D) as statements by the company's agent (COO) on a matter within the scope of the agency relationship (company finances, loan applications).
- **Mayersky's statements (Message 1):** Mayersky is a Fifth Third employee. If offered against Rowboat Creative and Mayersky was acting as Rowboat's agent in facilitating the loan, FRE 801(d)(2)(D) may apply. If not, his statements may be non-hearsay as verbal acts (initiating a loan application has independent legal significance) or admissible under FRE 803(6) if part of Fifth Third's business records.
- **Mendoza's questions (Message 2):** Not hearsay — questions are not assertive statements offered for the truth of the matter asserted. They are relevant to show the information requested and the context of the loan process.

### FRE 901 — Authentication: PASS
Authenticated through source database metadata ("mayersky_smoking_gun," Row ID 223), email header information (From, To, CC, Date, Subject), and the distinctive signature block containing JZ's cell phone number. Any party with personal knowledge (JZ, LG, Mayersky, or a Fifth Third records custodian) can authenticate. Alternatively, FRE 901(b)(4) — distinctive characteristics taken in conjunction with circumstances.

### FRE 1002 — Best Evidence Rule: PASS
The original email in native digital format is available from the "mayersky_smoking_gun" database. A printout or PDF is admissible as a duplicate under FRE 1003 unless a genuine question is raised about the original's authenticity or it would be unfair.

### FRE 602 — Personal Knowledge: PASS
JZ, as COO and co-founder, had personal knowledge of Rowboat Creative's annual sales, employee count, customer count, and lease obligations. His personal financial information was explicitly deferred to a separate communication, suggesting he distinguished between company knowledge (provided here) and personal knowledge (withheld pending separate delivery).

### Additional Considerations
- **FRE 106 — Rule of Completeness:** If only Message 3 (JZ's reply) is introduced, opposing counsel may demand the full thread including Mayersky's initiation and Mendoza's questions. The full thread should be introduced to avoid a completeness objection.
- **FRE 408 — Compromise Offers:** Not implicated. This is a loan application, not a settlement negotiation.

## Section 5: Strategic Notes

### Deposition Questions

1. **To Joe Zangrilli:** "In your March 31, 2020 email to Fifth Third, you represented annual sales of $2,406,195 for 2019. Can you walk me through how you arrived at that figure, and whether it matches the company's 2019 tax return as filed with the IRS?"

2. **To Lucas Guariglia:** "You were CC'd on Leonard Mayersky's email initiating the $100,000 Fundation loan application. Did you authorize this application? Did the operating agreement require unanimous member consent for debt of this magnitude?"

3. **To Leonard Mayersky:** "You described Rowboat Creative as 'our friends' when initiating the loan application. What was the nature of your relationship with Lucas Guariglia and Rowboat Creative? How many other loan applications did you initiate or facilitate on Rowboat's behalf, and were they all properly documented through Fifth Third's standard underwriting channels?"

4. **To Joe Zangrilli:** "You deferred your personal financial information to a separate communication. Was that separate communication ever sent? What personal financial information did you ultimately provide to Fifth Third, and was the loan approved?"

5. **To Lucas Guariglia:** "The source of this email was described as 'LG-and-Leonard-Mayersky-communicate-corporate-email-servers-no-JZ.' Why was JZ typically excluded from your communications with Mr. Mayersky?"

### Discovery Follow-Ups

- **Fifth Third subpoena (high priority):** Obtain the complete loan file for the Fundation application referenced in this email — application, underwriting notes, approval/denial, promissory note, and disbursement records. Determine whether this was a PPP loan (SBA Form 2483) or a conventional Fundation product.
- **Rowboat Creative financial records:** Cross-reference the $1,961,141 (2018) and $2,406,195 (2019) figures against (a) QuickBooks general ledger, (b) federal tax returns (Form 1065), (c) Illinois state tax filings, and (d) Fifth Third bank statements for the corresponding periods.
- **CARES Act / PPP nexus:** Request all Rowboat Creative PPP applications, SBA loan numbers, and forgiveness applications. Determine whether the "Fundation" loan was PPP-eligible and whether any PPP funds were commingled with or diverted from company accounts.
- **Mayersky communications:** Obtain all communications between Mayersky and any Rowboat Creative principal from 2019–2023 from the "mayersky_smoking_gun" database to establish the full scope of the banking relationship.

### Motion Practice

- **Summary Judgment (Accounting Claim):** If the financial figures in this email materially diverge from QuickBooks or tax records, this exhibit supports a partial summary judgment motion on the accounting claim, as the discrepancy would be established by documentary evidence.
- **Motion in Limine:** Anticipate and preempt any FRE 403 or hearsay objection by preparing a foundational brief establishing 801(d)(2)(A) admissibility and the low prejudice risk.
- **Spoliation:** If the "separate" personal financial communication referenced by JZ cannot be located in discovery, consider a spoliation inference or motion to compel.

### Timing / Sequence

The March 31, 2020 date — four days post-CARES Act — is strategically significant. The CARES Act authorized $349 billion in PPP loans on a first-come, first-served basis. This email places Rowboat Creative's principals at the front of the line, facilitated by a bank insider. If loan proceeds were subsequently diverted, the speed and insider access amplify the fraud narrative.

### Settlement Leverage

This exhibit is a single thread in a larger tapestry. Its settlement value lies in: (a) the verifiable financial figures that create an objective benchmark for accounting disputes, and (b) the Mayersky connection, which may expose Fifth Third to third-party liability or regulatory scrutiny — leverage that affects not just the inter-party dispute but the bank's willingness to cooperate in discovery.
