## Section 1: Evidence Summary

This email thread from late March / early April 2020 documents the Rowboat Creative principals — Joe Zangrilli (JZ) and Lucas Guariglia (LG) — coordinating with their Fifth Third Bank relationship manager Leonard Mayersky regarding the CARES Act / Paycheck Protection Program (PPP) loan application. The thread shows both JZ and LG jointly engaged in obtaining pandemic relief financing for the company, with JZ proactively applying for an SBA EIDL loan and both co-members coordinating on the forthcoming PPP application. Critically, this email establishes that JZ was actively involved in corporate financial management during this period, contradicting any narrative that LG alone handled banking relationships. The email is *exculpatory* for JZ on any claim that JZ was disengaged from business finances, and *neutral-to-supportive* of JZ's claim that he was a fully participating co-member in Rowboat Creative.

## Section 4: Admissibility Analysis (Federal Rules of Evidence)

**FRE 401/402 — Relevance:**
- This email is relevant to establishing JZ's participation and engagement in Rowboat Creative's financial management during the pre-dispute period. It makes it more probable that JZ was a fully involved co-member with access to banking relationships, which is a fact of consequence in any claim that LG excluded JZ from financial operations.

**FRE 403 — Prejudice / Confusion:**
- There is no risk of unfair prejudice. The email contains routine banking correspondence with no inflammatory content. Probative value substantially outweighs any risk of confusion or waste of time.

**FRE 801/802 — Hearsay:**
- Out-of-court statements by Leonard Mayersky (bank employee) regarding CARES Act procedures are being offered to show that both members received the same banking information, not for the truth of the CARES Act requirements themselves.
- Statements by JZ ("We already applied for the sba loan") — offered to show JZ's state of mind and action, admissible as a statement of a party-opponent under FRE 801(d)(2)(A).
- Statements by LG ("we should not be doing anything tomorrow to apply for CARES through any other platforms") — offered to show LG's state of mind and as a statement of a party-opponent under FRE 801(d)(2)(A).
- Statements by Mayersky regarding Fifth Third Bank's timeline — may be offered for the truth of the matter (that the bank was going live the following week), but qualify under FRE 803(3) (then-existing state of mind/plan) or as a business record under FRE 803(6).
