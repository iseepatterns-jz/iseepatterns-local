## 4. Admissibility Analysis

### 4.1 FRE 401/402 — Relevance

**Finding: ADMISSIBLE — Relevant**

EXH-0071 is plainly relevant under FRE 401. It tends to make the existence of a personal bank account opened by LG — concealed from JZ, during a period of alleged financial misconduct — more probable than it would be without the evidence. The account's existence is a fact of consequence in determining whether LG breached fiduciary duties, converted assets, or was unjustly enriched. The temporal proximity to account closures (subdirectory: "week before account closed") makes the relevance particularly acute.

**Caveat:** Relevance of the subdirectory name itself (which is metadata from the source locker, not the email) may require foundational testimony from the custodian of the Leonard Mayersky Locker database.

### 4.2 FRE 403 — Prejudice vs. Probative Value

**Finding: ADMISSIBLE — Probative Value Not Substantially Outweighed**

The probative value of EXH-0071 is high: it is a contemporaneous, automatically generated business record confirming LG's in-person account opening at a specific time and place. While the evidence is prejudicial to LG (it supports an inference of concealment and self-dealing), this is legitimate probative prejudice, not unfair prejudice. The danger of confusing the issues or misleading the jury is low — the document is straightforward. No FRE 403 bar.

**Risk:** If the "BANK_FRAUD_ACCOUNT_MANIPULATION" priority tag (pipeline metadata) is inadvertently disclosed to the jury, it could be unfairly prejudicial. This tag must be redacted (see Section 6).

### 4.3 FRE 801/802 — Hearsay

**Finding: ADMISSIBLE — Multiple Hearsay Exceptions Apply**

EXH-0071 is an out-of-court statement (the Fifth Third Bank email declaring that LG opened an account). However, several exceptions apply:

1. **FRE 803(6) — Business Records Exception:** The Visit Summary is an automatically generated record of a regularly conducted business activity (bank account opening), made at or near the time of the event by Fifth Third Bank, and kept in the ordinary course of Fifth Third's business. The foundational requirements can be satisfied through a records custodian affidavit or subpoena under FRE 902(11).

2. **FRE 803(7) — Absence of Business Records:** If Fifth Third has no record of JZ being an authorized signer or having any access to Account x4548, this absence is admissible to prove the account was concealed.

3. **FRE 801(d)(2) — Opposing Party Statement (if applicable):** The email is not LG's statement — it is Fifth Third's. However, if LG produced this email in discovery or it was found in his possession, it may be admissible as an adopted admission under 801(d)(2)(B) (manifested adoption of belief in its truth).

**Recommended Foundation:** Subpoena Fifth Third Bank for a certified copy under FRE 902(11) with a custodian declaration. This eliminates hearsay concerns entirely.

### 4.4 FRE 901 — Authentication

**Finding: AUTHENTICABLE**

EXH-0071 can be authenticated through multiple independent paths:

1. **FRE 901(b)(1) — Witness with Knowledge:** Robin Varghese (the Retail Personal Banker, NMLS #1932366) can testify that she opened the account for LG on January 31, 2023 and that the Visit Summary is the type of email Fifth Third sends.

2. **FRE 901(b)(3) — Comparison by Trier of Fact:** The email's content (specific account number, routing number, banker name, date) can be compared with Fifth Third's internal records.

3. **FRE 901(b)(4) — Distinctive Characteristics:** The email contains Fifth Third branding, the correct financial center address, banker NMLS ID, and the standard "Visit Summary" format — all distinctive characteristics of authentic Fifth Third communications.

4. **FRE 901(b)(7) — Public Records/Reports:** Not directly applicable, but Fifth Third's regulatory filings confirm the Dilworth branch existence.

5. **FRE 901(b)(9) — System/Process Evidence:** Fifth Third can describe its automated email generation system for Visit Summaries.

**Recommendation:** Obtain a stipulation from Fifth Third or serve a business records subpoena with an FRE 902(11) certification. If contested, call Robin Varghese as an authenticating witness.

### 4.5 FRE 1002 — Best Evidence Rule

**Finding: ORIGINAL OR DUPLICATE REQUIRED**

Under FRE 1002, the original or a duplicate of the Visit Summary email is required to prove its content. The source file (EXH-0071_LML_180_nodate.txt) is a plain-text extraction from the Leonard Mayersky Locker database. To satisfy the Best Evidence Rule:

- Obtain the **original .eml or .pdf** from the source database or from LG's email account (lucas@rowboatcreative.com).
- Alternatively, obtain a certified copy directly from Fifth Third Bank's records.
- The email is also likely preserved in LG's Fifth Third online banking message center, which Fifth Third can produce.

**Current Status:** The .txt extraction is a "secondary evidence" form. Under FRE 1004, secondary evidence is admissible if the original is lost or destroyed through no bad faith of the proponent — but it is far preferable to secure the original or a certified duplicate.

### 4.6 FRE 602 — Personal Knowledge

**Finding: SATISFIED FOR AUTHENTICATING WITNESSES**

FRE 602 requires that witnesses testify from personal knowledge. Robin Varghese has personal knowledge of the account opening (she is the named banker). A Fifth Third records custodian has personal knowledge of the bank's record-keeping systems. JZ lacks personal knowledge of this account (JZ_CCED: NO) — precisely the point the exhibit proves.

**Deposition Strategy:** Depose Robin Varghese to establish personal knowledge of: (a) LG's physical presence at the Dilworth branch on January 31, 2023; (b) LG's stated purpose for the account; (c) whether LG mentioned Rowboat Creative, JZ, or any business purpose; (d) identification documents LG presented.
