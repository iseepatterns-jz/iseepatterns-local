This exhibit captures the initiation of a $100,000 Fundation loan application by Rowboat Creative through Fifth Third Bank. It is significant for four reasons. ## 4. Admissibility Analysis

### 4.1 FRE 401/402 — Relevance

**Finding: ADMISSIBLE — Relevant**

EXH-0308 is plainly relevant under FRE 401. It tends to make the following facts of consequence more probable: (a) that a $100,000 Fundation loan application was initiated for Rowboat Creative on March 31, 2020; (b) that Joseph Zangrilli, in his capacity as COO, engaged directly with Fifth Third Bank regarding the application and provided notice to Lucas Guariglia; (c) that Leonard Mayersky had a non-arm's-length relationship with Rowboat Creative, characterizing the company as "our friends"; and (d) that LG and Mayersky had separate communications about corporate email servers excluding JZ. Each of these facts is material to the claims of fiduciary breach, breach of contract, accounting, and CFAA violations. The subdirectory-name evidence is relevant to intent and the existence of a parallel, exclusionary communications channel.

**Caveat:** The subdirectory name's relevance depends on foundational testimony establishing its origin (custodian-assigned folder name vs. investigator label). Without foundation, its probative value is limited, though the email content itself remains independently relevant.

### 4.2 FRE 403 — Prejudice vs. Probative Value

**Finding: ADMISSIBLE — Probative Value Not Substantially Outweighed**

The probative value of EXH-0308 is substantial. The email is a contemporaneous business communication capturing the initiation of a significant financial transaction. The subdirectory name, if properly authenticated, is directly probative of LG's intent to exclude JZ from email-server-related communications. While the evidence is prejudicial to LG (the subdirectory name suggests secretive conduct), this is legitimate probative prejudice, not unfair prejudice. There is minimal risk of confusing the issues or misleading the jury — the email is short, clear, and facially innocuous, with the subdirectory metadata providing the inculpatory dimension.

**Risk:** The `BANK_FRAUD_TRANSFERS` pipeline priority tag and other investigative metadata must be redacted from any trial exhibit (see Section 6). If presented without foundation for the subdirectory name, the exhibit is far less impactful but still admissible for its content.

### 4.3 FRE 801/802 — Hearsay

**Finding: ADMISSIBLE — Multiple Hearsay Exceptions and Non-Hearsay Uses Apply**

EXH-0308 contains two out-of-court statements:

**Thread Entry 1 (Mayersky to Mendoza):** "Our friends at Rowboat Creative needs to apply for our 100K Fundation loan." This is hearsay if offered to prove that Rowboat Creative actually needed the loan. However:
1. **FRE 803(3) — State of Mind:** Offered to show Mayersky's then-existing state of mind (his belief that Rowboat Creative was a "friend" and should apply), not the truth of the need. This is a non-hearsay use if offered to prove Mayersky's relationship with and attitude toward Rowboat Creative.
2. **FRE 803(6) — Business Records:** If offered as a Fifth Third Bank business record reflecting the initiation of a loan application, admissible under the business records exception with proper custodian foundation.
3. **Not offered for truth:** Much of the value of this statement is that it was made — the fact that a bank employee initiated a loan application for "our friends" is itself the relevant fact, regardless of whether Rowboat Creative actually "needed" the loan.

**Thread Entry 2 (Zangrilli to Mendoza):** "I am here to answer any questions. Let me know what you need!"
1. **FRE 801(d)(2)(A) — Opposing Party Statement:** If offered against JZ (e.g., by LG to show JZ's authorization and involvement), this is an opposing party's own statement and is not hearsay.
2. **FRE 801(d)(2)(C) or (D) — Authorized Agent:** JZ was acting as COO of Rowboat Creative; his statement is attributable to the LLC as an authorized agent statement.
3. **FRE 803(3) — State of Mind:** Offered to show JZ's cooperative intent and willingness to facilitate the loan.

**Subdirectory Name:** If offered for its truth (that LG and Mayersky communicated about email servers without JZ), it is hearsay unless: (a) it is a business record of the forensic collection process (FRE 803(6)), or (b) it is an adoptive admission if assigned by LG (FRE 801(d)(2)(B)). Foundation from the evidence custodian is essential.

### 4.4 FRE 901 — Authentication

**Finding: AUTHENTICABLE — Multiple Independent Paths**

EXH-0308 can be authenticated through several routes:

1. **FRE 901(b)(1) — Witness with Knowledge:** Leonard Mayersky, Haidy Mendoza, Joseph Zangrilli, or Lucas Guariglia can each testify that they sent, received, or were CC'd on this email thread and that it accurately reflects the March 31, 2020 communication.
2. **FRE 901(b)(3) — Comparison:** The email can be compared with other authenticated emails from the same period using the same `@rowboatcreative.com` and `@53.com` domains.
3. **FRE 901(b)(4) — Distinctive Characteristics:** The email contains JZ's distinctive signature block (cell 773.610.9104, COO | Co-founder title, Rowboat Creative branding), the In-Reply-To header linking it to Mayersky's message, and standard Fifth Third Bank confidentiality boilerplate — all characteristics confirming authenticity.
4. **FRE 901(b)(6) — Telephone Conversation (by analogy):** Zangrilli's rapid 16-minute response, sent to the person (Mayersky) who initiated the thread, addressing the topic raised (Fundation loan), shows it was sent by the person who would logically respond.

**Recommendation:** If Fifth Third and Rowboat Creative stipulate to the authenticity of business emails, authentication is straightforward. If contested, call JZ (or use his deposition) to authenticate his own email, and Fifth Third's records custodian to authenticate Mayersky's.

### 4.5 FRE 1002 — Best Evidence Rule

**Finding: ORIGINAL OR DUPLICATE REQUIRED**

Under FRE 1002, the original or a mechanically produced duplicate of the email is required to prove its content. The current source file (`EXH-0308_MSG_116_2020-03-31.txt`) is a plain-text extraction from the mayersky_smoking_gun database, preserving the body and key headers. To satisfy the Best Evidence Rule:

- Obtain the **original .eml file** referenced in the source path (`2020-03-31 12_15_37__Rowboat Creative_ _joe@rowboatcreative.com__Re_ Fun.eml`). This preserves all MIME headers, routing information, and digital signatures valuable for authentication.
- Alternatively, produce a certified copy from Fifth Third Bank's email archives or Rowboat Creative's email server.
- Under FRE 1004, if the original is shown to be lost or destroyed through no bad faith, the .txt extraction is admissible as secondary evidence — but the original is strongly preferred.

**Current Status:** The .eml original is referenced in the source path field and should be obtainable from the mayersky_smoking_gun database.
