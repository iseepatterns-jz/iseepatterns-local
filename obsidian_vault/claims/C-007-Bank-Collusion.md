# Claim C-007: Bank Collusion / Privacy Violation (Mayersky-Fifth Third)

## Summary
Fifth Third Bank representative Leonard Mayersky colluded with LG by: (1) forwarding JZ's personal banking documents to LG without JZ's consent; (2) facilitating LG's establishment of 8 new RBC accounts with JZ excluded; (3) providing preferential treatment to LG that enabled his consolidation of unilateral financial control. This constitutes a privacy violation under the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act and common law, as well as aiding and abetting LG's breach of fiduciary duty.

## Legal Basis
- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA), 15 U.S.C. § 6801: financial privacy protections
- Common law invasion of privacy / breach of confidence
- Aiding and abetting breach of fiduciary duty
- Potential OCC / CFPB regulatory violations

## Factual Allegations
1. Mayersky forwarded JZ's personal banking documents to LG without authorization
2. Mayersky facilitated LG opening 8 new RBC accounts — JZ excluded from all
3. Mayersky was present during Chase-to-53 transition (2020)
4. JZ reported Mayersky to Fifth Third on June 23, 2023 (EXH-0030)
5. 579 emails extracted from Mayersky smoking gun database
