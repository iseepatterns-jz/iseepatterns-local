# Claim 5: Unjust Enrichment

## Description

LG received substantial benefits at Rowboat Creative's expense under circumstances that make retention unjust. LG — as a 50% fiduciary — diverted customer contracts to his competing entity AWM, used Rowboat's bank accounts for side ventures, charged personal travel to the company credit card, received physical merchandise ordered under Rowboat's name at his personal residence, and benefited from JZ's uncompensated labor during the bank exclusion period. The constellation of benefits was received through active concealment (secret bank transition, separate accountant communications), during a fiduciary relationship, and while LG admitted his intent to "do less and make more." Even if LG were to argue good faith (which the evidence contradicts), the benefit he received at Rowboat's expense independently supports this claim — unjust enrichment does not require wrongful intent, only that retention is unjust.

## Legal Elements

1. **Defendant received a benefit** — LG (and his entities/family) received money, contracts, goods, and services
2. **At plaintiff's expense** — Benefits came from Rowboat Creative LLC, in which JZ owns 50%
3. **Unjust for defendant to retain benefit without payment** — Fiduciary position, active concealment, admission of unequal effort intent
