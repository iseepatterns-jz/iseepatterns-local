# Claim C-009: Defamation / False Statements to Third Parties

## Summary
LG made defamatory statements about JZ to multiple third parties: (1) told Fifth Third Bank that JZ "stole $8K" from the company — the $8K was actually two months of agreed $4K/month compensation (EXH-0026); (2) told contractor Abel Rodriguez that JZ "wants to shut down," is "not right in his head," and is "taking customer money" (EXH-0048). These statements constitute defamation per se as they impute criminal conduct and dishonesty in JZ's trade.

## Legal Basis
- Defamation per se: statements imputing criminal conduct, professional incompetence, or dishonesty
- Illinois recognizes defamation per se with presumed damages

## Factual Allegations
1. LG's lawyer told Fifth Third Bank that JZ stole $8K (EXH-0026)
2. LG first denied, then admitted his lawyer told the bank this
3. The $8K was two months of agreed $4K/month compensation — not theft
4. LG told Abel Rodriguez that JZ "wants to shut down," is "not right in his head" (EXH-0048)
5. Statements caused actual harm: bank account closures, contractor mistrust

- Bank account closures triggered by theft allegation
